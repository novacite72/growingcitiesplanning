목적: 단순 발표가 아니라 VISION 초안에 대한 국제 전문가 동료검토(peer review) — 4대 교차주제로 영향평가하고 맹점(blind spots)을 드러내며 명확한 권고안을 도출.

VISION(VISIO) 지역계획

  • 성격: 「혁신적 녹색전환을 위한 주제별(thematic) 광역토지이용계획」 — 기존 계획을 보완·부분대체하는 단계계획, 전략적·지시적(축척 1:250,000)
  • 범위·일정: 우시마 전역 26개 지자체 · 2024–2027 수립 · 목표연도 2050 · 초안 공람(2026.3.11–5.8) 후 2027 승인 예정
  • 위상: 국가·광역적 중요사항을 다루고 지자체 상세계획의 지침 — "유연성·일반성" 지향
"확장이 아닌 내부로 성장(Growing inwards)" — 기존 시가지·인프라 기반 고밀화 + 강한 중심지 + 대중교통·보행·자전거 서비스망 + 생태네트워크·수자원·재생에너지 강화

전망: 인구 180만 → 2050 최대 220만(+55만), 일자리 86.6만 → 약 100만(+29만) · 2030 기후중립 후 탄소 네거티브(2005년比 2030년 배출 80% 감축)

3. VISION 계획 6대 주제

① 에너지 — 태양광(50ha·1MWp 기준), 원자력(Loviisa+SMR 1,500MWth 미만), 지열·폐열(데이터센터 폐열회수 의무), 송전망 400/110kV·EstLink 3·수소망
② 자연·탄소격리 — 보호구역 3,337ha, 녹색연결(도시 300m/외곽 500–1,000m), 생물다양성 중요지(LUO), 생태보상·완화위계, 탄소흡수원(산림 52.1만ha)
③ 산업생산·순환경제·토양관리 — 약 25개 클러스터 단일 개발원칙지정(Joddböle 청정수소·녹색강, Sköldvik 정유·순환경제 등), 심수항=수소·청정산업 적지
④ 물관리·수자원 — 광역 물공급지·정책(안보상 핵심 물공급지 지도 비표기), 지하수 규정
⑤ 물류·장거리 여객교통 — 공항철도·동부철도·탈린터널, Vuosaari·Inkoo 항, 대체연료 급유소, 환승거점(TEN-T)
⑥ 국가방위(신규) — 안보상황 반영, 지도 최소표기(4개 EP구역), 사격장·소음/보호구역, 이중용도 교통망

3대 상위목표: 기후·생물다양성 대응 · 지속가능·혁신 경쟁력 · 기능성·회복력·안전 (지역비전 "유럽에서 가장 환경스마트·혁신·행복한 지역")

4. 4대 교차주제 — 영향평가 관점

6개 주제를 하나의 지속가능 녹색전환 프레임으로 묶어 계획해법을 평가·개선하는 "감사자(auditor)" 역할. 워크숍은 이 중 하나를 골라 검토.

① 기후변화 완화·적응 — 목표: 2030 기후중립 후 탄소 네거티브 + 회복력. 완화는 강하게 지원, 적응 영향은 긍정적이나 제한적.
② 안보·회복력·공급안정 — 개별해법이 아닌 총체적 토지이용으로 핵심기능 보호·시스템 분산·리스크 예측. 인프라-산업-자연 연계.
③ 정의·사회적 수용성 — 분배정의+인정정의. 지자체별 영향평가·취약집단·보상·주민 지역지식·협의를 넘은 공동생산(co-production). "공정성이 수용성을 높이나 보장하진 않는다".
④ 혁신적 녹색전환 — 유연·예측가능 프레임으로 혁신환경 강화. 국제인재·경로의존성(교통)·데이터센터 입지(폐열·지역난방 인접)·항만 혁신허브 지적.

🌸 영향평가 꽃(Impact Assessment Flower)

핵심 시각화 도구 — 안쪽 원=6개 주제가 교차주제를 이행하는 역할, 바깥 꽃잎=개별 계획지정의 영향 정도를 색으로 표현. 척도: 영향없음/미미/가능케 함(Enabling)/강함/상충(Contradictory)/부정.

  • 기후 완화: 자연·탄소격리=STRONG+, 에너지=+, 물류·산업=상충(철도↓배출 vs 신규도로·건설↑)
  • 기후 적응: 자연·탄소격리=STRONG+, 산업=상충(불투수면·열섬), 다수 주제 영향없음 → 적응이 완화보다 약함을 시각화

5. 워크숍 과제 · 중심 질문

"현재·미래의 도전에 더 잘 대응하기 위해, VISION 지역계획의 영향평가에서 특별히 주목해야 할 것은 무엇인가?"
  1. 선택 관점을 지역계획·VISION 맥락에서 정의
  2. 영향평가에서 주목할 핵심 이슈·영향·트레이드오프 식별
  3. 평가·계획을 강화할 권고안 1~3개 도출
  4. 산출물: 짧은 서면 요약(Teams 기록) → 전체세션 발표

혁신적 녹색전환 — 그룹 권고안(예시)

교차주제 ④ 혁신적 녹색전환 관점의 그룹 권고안 초안(영향평가·계획 강화용).

  1. 경로의존성 완화·미래유연성 확보 — 교통·에너지 등 대규모 인프라의 경로의존성(path dependency)이 급진적 혁신을 저해하므로, 계획에 유연지대(flexibility zones)·단계적 전환경로·대안노선 옵션을 명시하고, 영향평가에 혁신 잠금(lock-in) 리스크 지표를 추가.
  2. 데이터센터·수소 등 신흥 입지수요의 전략적 유도 — 데이터센터를 폐열회수·지역난방망 인접지로 유도하는 입지원칙을 계획지정으로 구체화하고, 심수항·산업클러스터를 수소·이퓨얼·녹색강 혁신허브로 프로파일링(폐열·전력·용수 연계 정량평가).
  3. 국제인재·혁신생태계 접근성 강화 — 대중교통·주거·연구클러스터(대학·R&D) 접근성을 혁신 KPI로 설정하고, 스마트 특성화(smart specialisation)와 연계한 클러스터 거버넌스(지자체·기업·대학 공동 프로파일링)를 계획 절차에 제도화.

※ 영향평가 유의: 혁신은 "여건 조성(enabling)" 성격이라 낙관 편향 위험 → 실현조건·리스크·모니터링 지표를 함께 평가.

6. 계획 절차 · 일정

  • 2023 — 이해관계자 조사로 필요성 도출
  • 2024.5 — 지역이사회, 5개 주제 단계계획 착수 결정 (이후 국방 6번째 주제 추가)
  • 2025 — 참여·평가계획(PAS) 공람(56건 의견)·계획원칙 승인·1차 당국협의
  • 2026.3.11–5.8 — 초안 공람(지도 5종 + 기후영향평가 부속서)
  • 제안(proposal) 단계 — 단일노선 확정, Natura 2000 영향평가(구역 내 100개소·약 18만 ha)
  • 2027 — 지역의회(Assembly) 승인 예정

변수: 토지이용법(Land Use Act) 개정(2026 발효 예상)이 절차·지정 용어에 영향 · 배출 2005년比 2030년 80% 감축

💡 서울·수도권 광역계획 시사점

  • "내부로 성장" 원칙 — 포화 시가지의 압축·재생 정당화. "이미 사용된 땅의 효율적 재활용" 논리는 수도권 광역계획에 직접 이식 가능.
  • 교차주제 영향평가 매트릭스 — 부문 나열이 아닌 4대 주제로 가로질러 평가·감사. 수도권정비계획의 통합성·소통(영향평가 꽃) 강화.
  • 정의·수용성 제도화 — 분배+인정정의, 지자체별 평가·보상·공동생산. 입지갈등이 첨예한 서울에 초기 개방대화·취약집단 배려의 제도화 시사.
  • 안보·공급안정을 토지이용에 통합 — 핵심 물공급·데이터센터·에너지망을 회복력 축으로. 반도체·데이터센터·전력망 안보가 부상하는 한국에 선제 참고.
  • 광역-기초 역할분담 — "프레임은 광역, 결정은 지자체"의 조정 부족 비판 → 자율성 존중과 광역 조정력의 균형 교훈.
  • 신흥 입지수요 사전 유도 — 폐열활용 데이터센터·수소/이퓨얼 허브를 계획단계에서 선제 유도·조정.

Mobile Workshop 13 (MW13) — Regional Planning for the Green Transition

Helsinki-Uusimaa Regional Council · International peer review of the VISION regional land-use plan draft
WPSC 2026 · Wed 1 July 2026 · Junyoung Choi, The Seoul Institute · Contact: Ilona Mansikka (uudenmaanliitto.fi)

MW13 invites international planning experts to conduct an impact assessment and peer review of the draft "VISION – Thematic Regional Plan for an Innovative Green Transition" of the Helsinki-Uusimaa Regional Council, from the standpoint of four cross-cutting themes, and to produce clear recommendations for developing the plan.

Purpose: not a mere presentation but an international expert peer review of the VISION draft — assessing impacts through four cross-cutting themes, surfacing blind spots, and drawing clear recommendations.

A metropolitan region in southern Finland (Helsinki, Espoo, Vantaa, Kauniainen, etc.). Land area 9,440 km². Three-tier hierarchy (4 core → 14 Helsinki region → 26 Uusimaa). Large urban-rural gap (Helsinki ~684,600 ↔ smallest, Pukkila ~1,750).

The VISION (VISIO) regional plan

  • Character: a thematic regional land-use plan "for an innovative green transition" — a phased plan that complements and partly replaces existing plans; strategic and indicative (scale 1:250,000).
  • Scope · schedule: all 26 Uusimaa municipalities · prepared 2024–2027 · target year 2050 · draft on public display (11 Mar–8 May 2026), approval expected 2027.
  • Status: addresses nationally/regionally significant matters and steers local detailed plans — aiming for "flexibility and generality".
"Growing inwards, not outwards" — densification on existing built-up areas and infrastructure + stronger centres + public-transport/walking/cycling service networks + reinforced ecological networks, water resources and renewable energy.

Outlook: population 1.8M → up to 2.2M by 2050 (+550k), jobs 866k → ~1M (+290k) · climate-neutral by 2030 then climate-negative (emissions −80% vs 2005 by 2030).

3. The six themes of the VISION plan

① Energy — solar (thresholds 50 ha / 1 MWp), nuclear (Loviisa + SMR under 1,500 MWth), geothermal/waste heat (data-centre waste-heat recovery required), transmission 400/110 kV · EstLink 3 · hydrogen network
② Nature & carbon sequestration — protected areas 3,337 ha, green connections (300 m urban / 500–1,000 m rural), biodiversity hotspots (LUO), ecological compensation & mitigation hierarchy, carbon sinks (forest 521,000 ha)
③ Industrial production, circular economy & soil management — ~25 clusters as a single development-principle designation (Joddböle clean hydrogen/green steel, Sköldvik refining/circular economy, etc.); deep-water ports as prime sites for hydrogen/clean industry
④ Water supply & water resources — regionally significant water sources/policies (key water sources omitted from the map for security), groundwater provisions
⑤ Logistics & long-distance passenger transport — Airport Rail Link, East Rail Line, Tallinn Tunnel, Vuosaari/Inkoo ports, alternative-fuel stations, interchange hubs (TEN-T)
⑥ National defence (new) — reflecting the changed security situation; minimal mapping (4 EP zones), firing ranges, noise/protection zones, dual-use transport networks

Three overarching goals: climate & biodiversity · sustainable/innovative competitiveness · functionality, resilience & security (regional vision: "the most environmentally smart, most innovative and happiest region in Europe").

4. The four cross-cutting themes — assessment lenses

They tie the six themes into one sustainable green-transition frame, acting as an "auditor" that evaluates and improves planning solutions. The workshop examines one of them.

① Climate change mitigation & adaptation — goal: climate-neutral by 2030, then carbon-negative + resilience. Strongly supports mitigation; adaptation impact positive but limited.
② Security, resilience & security of supply — a holistic land-use approach (not single solutions) protects key functions, diversifies systems, anticipates risks; links infrastructure-industry-nature.
③ Justice & social acceptance — distributive + recognitional justice. Municipality-level assessment, vulnerable groups, compensation, residents' local knowledge, and co-production beyond mere consultation. "Fairness increases acceptance but does not guarantee it."
④ Innovative green transition — a flexible, predictable frame strengthens the innovation environment. Panel notes: access to international talent, path dependency (transport), data-centre siting (near waste-heat/district-heating), ports as innovation hubs.

🌸 The Impact Assessment Flower

The key visualisation — the inner ring shows how each of the six themes delivers the cross-cutting themes; the outer petals show each designation's degree of impact, coloured by scale: No impact / Minor / Enabling / Strong / Contradictory / Negative.

  • Mitigation: nature/carbon = STRONG+, energy = +, logistics/industry = contradictory (rail cuts emissions vs. new roads/construction raise them).
  • Adaptation: nature/carbon = STRONG+, industry = contradictory (impervious surfaces/heat islands), many themes "no impact" → visualising that adaptation is weaker than mitigation.

5. Workshop task · central question

"To respond better to present and future challenges, what should the impact assessment of the VISION regional plan pay particular attention to?"
  1. Define the chosen lens in the context of regional planning and the VISION plan.
  2. Identify the key issues, impacts and trade-offs to watch in the assessment.
  3. Draw 1–3 recommendations to strengthen the assessment and the plan.
  4. Output: a short written summary (recorded in Teams) → present in the plenary.

Innovative Green Transition — group recommendations (draft)

Draft group recommendations from the cross-cutting lens ④ Innovative Green Transition (to strengthen the assessment and plan).

  1. Reduce path dependency, secure future flexibility — as path dependency in large infrastructure (transport, energy) hampers radical innovation, designate flexibility zones, staged transition pathways and alternative-alignment options, and add a lock-in risk indicator to the impact assessment.
  2. Strategically steer emerging siting demand (data centres, hydrogen) — turn data-centre siting principles (near waste-heat recovery / district-heating networks) into concrete plan designations, and profile deep-water ports and industrial clusters as hydrogen/e-fuel/green-steel innovation hubs (quantifying waste-heat/power/water synergies).
  3. Strengthen access to international talent and innovation ecosystems — set accessibility (public transport, housing, university/R&D clusters) as an innovation KPI, and institutionalise cluster governance (joint municipality-business-university profiling) linked to smart specialisation in the planning process.

Note: innovation is "enabling," risking optimism bias → also assess enabling conditions, risks and monitoring indicators.

6. Planning process · timeline

  • 2023 — need established through stakeholder surveys.
  • May 2024 — Regional Board decides to start a five-theme phased plan (national defence later added as a sixth theme).
  • 2025 — Participation & Assessment plan (PAS) on display (56 comments), planning principles approved, first authority consultation.
  • 11 Mar–8 May 2026 — draft on public display (5 map annexes + climate impact assessment annex).
  • Proposal stage — single alignments fixed, Natura 2000 assessment (100+ sites, ~180,000 ha in the area).
  • 2027 — approval expected by the Regional Council Assembly.

Variable: the Land Use Act reform (in force 2026) affects the process and designation terminology · emissions −80% vs 2005 by 2030.

💡 Implications for Seoul / the Capital Region

  • "Growing inwards" — justifies compaction/regeneration of a saturated urban fabric; the "reuse of already-used land" logic is directly transferable to Seoul metropolitan planning.
  • Cross-cutting assessment matrix — evaluating/auditing across four themes rather than listing sectors; strengthens the integration and communication (the Impact Assessment Flower) of capital-region plans.
  • Institutionalising justice & acceptance — distributive + recognitional justice, municipality-level assessment, compensation, co-production; relevant to Seoul's acute siting conflicts.
  • Integrating security & supply into land use — treating water sources, data centres and energy grids as a resilience axis; timely for Korea amid semiconductor/data-centre/grid security concerns.
  • Regional-vs-local roles — the critique that "the framework is regional but decisions are local, with insufficient coordination" is a lesson in balancing local autonomy with regional steering.
  • Pre-steering emerging siting demand — proactively guiding waste-heat data centres and hydrogen/e-fuel hubs at the planning stage.
