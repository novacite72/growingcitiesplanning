Helsinki-Uusimaa Regional Council · WPSC 2026 · 1 July 2026

The lens

What "Innovative Green Transition" means

  • Using strategic land use to enable innovation
  • Drives innovation in energy, industry, circular economy and resource management
  • Offers a flexible, predictable frame → Uusimaa as an innovation environment
  • One of four cross-cutting themes that audit the whole plan
What we assessed

Does the VISION draft enable innovation?

  • The plan enables innovation rather than creating it directly
  • Impact scale: No impact → Minor → Enabling → Strong → Contradictory → Negative
  • ⚠️ The "enabling" character risks optimism bias — a best-case reading
Key issues — findings

Five things that decide innovation

  • Path dependency — big infrastructure (esp. transport) locks in and slows radical innovation
  • Data centres — siting must connect to waste-heat recovery / district heating
  • International talent — accessibility (transit, housing, R&D) is decisive
  • Ports — deep-water ports as hydrogen / clean-industry hubs
  • Assessment leans best-case → needs enabling conditions & risks
Trade-offs

Tensions to manage

  • Flexibility ↔ certainty — an indicative frame vs. bankable designations
  • Densification ("growing inwards") ↔ land for new industry & energy
  • Regional frame ↔ local decisions — a coordination gap
Conclusion

Three recommendations

to strengthen the assessment and the plan — from lens ④ Innovative Green Transition
1 De-risk lock-in  ·  2 Steer new demand  ·  3 Open access
1

Reduce path dependency,
secure future flexibility

  • Designate flexibility zones, staged transition pathways and alternative alignments
  • Add a "lock-in risk" indicator to the impact assessment
Why — infrastructure lock-in (especially transport) is the main brake on radical innovation.
2

Strategically steer emerging
siting demand

  • Turn siting principles (waste-heat / district-heating adjacency) into concrete designations
  • Profile deep-water ports & clusters as hydrogen / e-fuel / green-steel hubs
  • Quantify waste-heat – power – water synergies
Why — capture data-centre & hydrogen demand as a regional innovation asset, not a burden.
3

Strengthen access to talent
& innovation ecosystems

  • Set accessibility (transit, housing, university/R&D) as an innovation KPI
  • Institutionalise cluster governance — joint municipality–business–university profiling
  • Link to smart specialisation
Why — innovation follows accessible people and connected ecosystems.
In short

Don't just enable —
de-risk & steer innovation

  • Assess enabling conditions, risks & monitoring — counter optimism bias
  • A flexible regional frame + proactive steering = a resilient innovation region
