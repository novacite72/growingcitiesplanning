VISION 계획 표시·규정

헬싱키-우시마 지역의회(Uudenmaan liitto)의 VISION – 혁신적 녹색전환(Uusimaa 제5차 단계별 지역계획) 초안에 담긴 계획 표시(도면 지정)와 그에 딸린 계획 규정을 정리한 자료입니다. 지역계획은 공간계획 중 가장 일반적(개괄적) 수준으로, 그 개괄성은 계획의 내용·표현방식·해석 모두에 적용됩니다. 아래 표시와 규정은 그대로 적용되는 것이 아니라 보다 상세한 하위계획에서 구체화되어야 합니다. 공람기간: 2026년 3월 11일 ~ 5월 8일.

계획 표시 유형(범례)

  • 지역용도지정(area reservation) — 토지이용의 1차적 목적을 나타내며 상호배타적. 동일 구역에 두 개의 지역용도지정이 동시에 유효할 수 없음. (예: 보호구역)
  • 지점지정(site designation) — 계획 축척 대비 작은 구역 또는 상세계획 단계에서 구체적 경계 획정이 적절한 구역. 다른 표시와 중첩 가능. (예: 에너지 공급 구역)
  • 선형지정(line designation) — 교통 연결 및 기술 인프라망 등을 표시. 점선의 지시적(indicative) 선형은 위치·실현이 불확실한 연결·망을 나타냄. (예: 송전선)
  • 속성지정(attribute designation) — 보전하거나 제약을 고려해야 할 최소 지역적 중요성의 특별 성격을 지닌 세부구역을 표시. (예: 가치 있는 지질 형성물)
  • 개발원칙지정(development principle) — 지역발전에 중요하나 지역계획의 상세수준상 정밀한 용도지정·선형으로 표현하기 어려운 구역·연결을 원칙적 수준에서 표시. (예: 녹지축 필요)

일부 표시는 토지이용법(AKL) 제33조에 따른 개발제한(development restriction) 대상이며, 해당 여부는 각 규정에 명시됩니다. 보호구역은 토지이용법 제33조 및 건축법(2025.1.1 시행) 제49조에 따른 조건부 개발제한 대상입니다.

에너지

지정(표시)계획 규정
송전선전압 110kV 이상의 기존 송전선과 기존 송전회랑 내에서 개발될 연결을 나타내는 선형지정. 토지이용 계획 시 송전선 안전거리에 관한 규정을 고려해야 함. 토지이용법(AKL) 제33조에 따른 개발제한 대상.
송전선의 지시적 선형새로운 송전회랑에 계획된 전압 110kV 이상 송전선의 지시적 노선을 나타내는 점선지정. 상세계획에서 노선 실현 가능성을 확보해야 함. 위치와 기술적 실현은 상세계획 및 정식 인허가 절차에서 구체화됨.
송전선 연결 필요전압 110kV 이상 송전선의 연결 또는 개발 필요를 나타내는 개발원칙지정. 위치와 기술적 실현은 상세계획에서 구체화됨.
에너지 공급 구역 EN/h전력공급 안정에 필수적인 신규 400kV 변전소의 필요와 지시적 위치를 나타내는 지점지정. 해당 구역 토지이용 계획 시 변전소의 실현 조건과 확장 가능성을 확보해야 함. 정확한 위치와 용도지정 범위는 상세계획에서 결정.
에너지 생산 구역 EN/t에너지 생산을 위한 구역을 나타내는 지점지정. 계획·실현 시 중대한 환경 교란을 기술적 해결책과 충분한 완충지대로 방지해야 함. 에너지 생산에 기여하는 시설·건물·구조물과 관련 연구개발 건물·구조물을 위해 유보됨. 원자력발전소와 핵폐기물 임시저장시설이 위치할 수 있으며, 임시숙소·수처리 관련 시설·항만 운영 등 지원 기능도 가능. 계획 시 방사선·원자력안전청(STUK)에 의견제출 기회 부여. 정확한 위치·범위는 상세계획에서 결정.
원자력발전소 지시적 보호구역대원자력발전소 보호구역대의 대략적 위치를 나타내는 속성지정. 정확한 위치·범위는 STUK의 현행 규정과 개별 결정에 따라 후속 사업계획·인허가에서 결정. 보호구역대 내에는 신규 밀집주거, 병원, 인구 밀집시설, 원전사고 영향을 받을 수 있는 중요 생산활동을 계획할 수 없음. 휴양주택·여가활동 배치 시 적절한 구조작업 여건이 손상되지 않도록 보장. 계획 시 STUK와 구조당국에 의견제출 기회 부여.
수소 수송관의 지시적 선형국가 수소 인프라의 일부인 수소 수송관의 지시적 위치를 나타내는 점선지정. 위치·기술적 실현은 상세계획·인허가에서 구체화. 다른 중대 영향 토지이용과 부지 유보를 조정하고, 기술·경제적으로 실현 가능한 노선이 확보되도록 보장. 상세계획에서 환경영향·토지이용 불이익을 최소화하기 위해 기존 인프라 회랑을 우선 활용.
수소 수송관 연결 필요국가 수소 인프라의 일부인 수소 수송관의 연결 필요를 나타내는 개발원칙지정. 위치·기술적 실현은 상세계획에서 구체화.
주 가스 수송관기존 고압 가스 수송관을 나타내는 선형지정. 천연가스·바이오가스·합성메탄·수소 및 그 혼합물의 수송용. 토지이용 계획 시 가스 수송관의 안전 및 안전거리 규정을 고려. 토지이용법(AKL) 제33조에 따른 개발제한 대상.

산업생산, 순환경제 및 골재공급

지정(표시)계획 규정
산업생산·물류·골재공급·순환경제 집적지도시기능 개발지대 외부에 위치한, 지역적으로 중요한 산업생산·물류·골재공급·순환경제 활동의 대규모 집적을 나타내는 개발원칙지정. 운영의 성격·규모상 중대한 환경영향을 야기할 수 있는 활동을 위해 지정됨. 상세계획에서 녹색·청정전환 사업, 산업생산시설, 위험화학물질 취급시설, 물류센터, 물류집약 산업 및 지원기능, 골재공급 관련 활동, 순환경제·폐기물관리, 청정 잉여토사 최종처분장, 수소생산·활용 사업, 이산화탄소 포집 사업 등을 지정 가능. 위치·범위는 상세계획에서 지속가능한 공간·정주구조의 기능적 일부가 되도록 결정. 중대한 환경 교란을 상세계획에서 검토하고, 기술적 해결책·완충지대로 저감. 신규 주거 등 부적합 용도는 지정 불가. 대형교통 공간 확보. 주변 환경·자연·문화역사·경관 특성 고려. 우수관리·극한기상 대비에 유의.

자연 및 탄소격리

지정(표시)계획 규정
보호구역자연보전법에 따라 보호되거나 보호될 예정인 구역을 나타내는 지역용도지정. 국립공원, 엄정자연보호구역, 기타 자연보전구역, 지역적으로 가치 있는 자연구역, 보전프로그램 포함지 및 자연보전법에 따른 이행이 결정된 Natura 2000 구역 포함. 기존 지역계획의 유효한 보호구역 지정과 함께 최소 5ha·폭 200m 초과의 구역을 대상으로 함. 당국이 그 관리 토지에 대해 자연보전법 외의 방식으로 보호결정한 구역도 포함 가능. 정확한 경계·보호규정은 당국 결정에 명시. 토지이용법(AKL) 제33조 및 건축법 제49조에 따른 조건부 개발제한 대상(토지이용법 제30조 보호규정 대상 아님). 보호의 근거가 된 자연·환경 가치를 위태롭게·훼손할 수 있는 어떠한 조치도 계획 불가.
녹지축 필요지역 생태·휴양 네트워크 중 상세 토지이용계획에서 고려가 필요하다고 식별된 연결 필요를 나타내는 개발원칙지정. 정확한 위치나 지상 폭은 나타내지 않음. 상세계획에서 해당 연결을 종(species)의 이동 보장을 우선하고 경관·자연 가치를 유지하며 필요시 휴양이용을 지원하도록 확보. 녹색·청색 인프라의 질과 무교란성 확보, 차선책으로 환경교란 관리. 연결 기능이 약화되는 경우 저감조치와 등가 연결의 대체 확보·강화 조치를 제시. 정확한 위치 결정 시 광역 생태·휴양 네트워크 일부로 기능할 여건 입증.
녹지축지역 생태 네트워크 기능에 결정적이며 대체 위치가 없는 연결을 나타내는 개발원칙지정. 상세계획에서 종의 이동 보장을 우선하고 경관·자연 가치 유지, 필요시 휴양이용 지원하도록 확보. 녹색·청색 인프라의 질·무교란성 확보, 차선책으로 환경교란 관리. 정확한 위치 결정 시 광역 생태·휴양 네트워크 일부로 기능할 여건 입증.
생태 상쇄구역자연보전법 제104조에 따라 결정된 상쇄구역을 나타내는 지역용도지정 또는 이에 상응하는 지점지정(50ha 미만은 지점지정). 1차 토지이용 목적은 자연보전법에 따른 생태적 상쇄이나 여러 토지이용 형태가 병존 가능. 일반원칙상 생성된 자연가치가 약화되어서는 안 됨. 상세 토지이용 제약은 상쇄구역 설정 결정에 명시. 산림법 적용에는 영향 없음. 상세계획에서 관련 당국 결정의 제약을 고려.
생태 개발지대자연보전법에 따른 생태 상쇄구역 지정 또는 자연상태 개선에 특히 적합한 구역을 나타내는 개발원칙지정 또는 상응 지점지정(50ha 미만은 지점지정). 잠재력을 나타낼 뿐 생태상쇄·자연복원 이행 의무를 부과하지 않음. 여러 토지이용 형태 병존 가능. 농림활동을 제약하지 않고, 산림법 적용에 영향 없으며, 일부 구역의 주거·경제활동 개발을 배제하지 않음. 상세계획에서 자연가치 생성 기회를 검토.
생물다양성 특별 중요구역(luo)국제적으로 중요한 조류구역(IBA), 국가적으로 중요한 조류구역(FINIBA), 핀란드 생태적으로 중요한 수중 해양자연구역(EMMA)을 나타내는 속성지정. 여러 토지이용 형태 병존 가능. 1차 생산 이용을 제한하지 않음. 지정 근거인 자연가치의 보전과 자연상태 개선을 촉진하는 방식으로 토지이용을 계획·이행.
휴양구역공공 휴양·야외활동을 위한 50ha 초과 구역을 나타내는 지역용도지정. 통상 국가·지자체·우시마 휴양지협회 소유·관리 토지에 위치. 구역 내 기존 노선·구조물 및 정주구조 통합에 필요한 것을 포함. 토지이용법(AKL) 제33조에 따른 개발제한 대상. 공공 휴양·야외활동을 위해 유보. 상세계획에서 휴양이용 여건, 접근성, 적절한 서비스시설, 환경가치를 확보. 환경질, 생태네트워크 내 위치, 생물다양성 중요성에 특히 유의. 공공 휴양이용에 기여하는 건물·구조물 설치 가능. 조사에 근거해 지역 노선·기술 인프라를 지정 가능하며, 휴양연결의 무장애·안전한 연속성 확보.
산림 우세구역(광대·연속·생태네트워크 중요)주로 임업에 이용되며 지역 생태네트워크에 중요한, 크고 연속된 산림구역을 나타내는 지역용도지정. 1차 이용 외 농업·분산형 농촌개발 등 병용 가능. 임업 및 기타 농촌생업의 운영·개발 여건을 확보. 구역 규모 대비 광범위·영구·장기적 이동장벽을 만드는 타 용도로 인한 파편화를 피해 구역의 완결성을 보전. 영향평가에 근거해 정주구조 통합에 필요한 지역 노선·기술 인프라 지정 가능. 불가피한 노선 계획 시 생태연결의 무장애·안전한 연속성을 최대한 확보.

지자체 기술서비스 구역지자체 기술서비스(특히 상하수도)에 기여하는 시설 구역을 나타내는 지점지정. 정확한 위치·범위는 상세계획에서 결정. 계획·실현 시 중대한 환경교란을 기술적 해결책·완충지대로 방지. 주변 자연가치를 약화시켜서는 안 됨. 주변 휴양이용, 생태네트워크·휴양노선의 연속성과 구역 외 연결을 확보. 시설은 이동장벽을 최소화하도록 계획하고, 시설 내 교통동선을 주변 교통망과 통합.

국방

지정(표시)계획 규정
국방군 구역공공 접근이 제한되는 국방군 상시 사용 구역을 나타내는 지역용도지정. 해당 구역·지점은 국방군 사용을 위해 유보.
국방군 소음지대사격·폭파 소음이 55dB LRden을 초과하거나 사격장 소음이 65dB LAImax를 초과하는, 국방군 활동으로 인한 소음지대를 나타내는 속성지정. 현행 활동과 기존 건물의 충전개발·대수선을 허용. 신규 주택, 별장, 요양·교육시설, 휴양·야영 구역, 기타 소음민감 기능은 배치 불가. 구역 이용 계획 시 국방군에 의견제출 기회 부여.
안전지대위험 또는 중대 교란을 야기하는 인근 활동으로 인해 이용을 제한해야 하는 구역을 나타내는 속성지정. 정부령 1101/2015 및 군용 폭발물 규정에 근거. 안전지대로 획정된 구역 내 신규 건물 배치 불가. 토지이용 영향 조치 및 건물 용도변경 시 국방군 의견을 요청해야 하며, 국방군은 해당 구역의 토지이용 변경·조치를 제한할 수 있음.
국방군 사격장 구역국방군이 사용하는 사격장을 나타내는 지점지정. 해당 구역은 국방군 사격장 운영을 위해 유보. 상세계획·이용 시 사격장 소음이 주변에 미치는 영향을 고려. 중대한 환경교란을 기술적 해결책·완충지대로 방지.

VISION – Plan Markings and Regulations

This document sets out the plan designations (map markings) and their associated planning provisions in the draft of the VISION – Innovative Green Transition, Uusimaa 5th Regional Phasing Plan by the Helsinki-Uusimaa Regional Council (Uudenmaan liitto). A regional plan is the most general level of spatial planning, and this generality applies to its content, presentation and interpretation. The markings and provisions below must not be applied as such but must be further specified in more detailed planning. Public consultation: 11 March – 8 May 2026.

Types of plan designation (legend)

  • Area reservation designation — indicates the primary purpose of land use and is mutually exclusive; two area reservation designations cannot be valid simultaneously for the same area. (e.g. protected areas)
  • Site designation — for areas small relative to the plan scale, or where precise delineation is appropriate only at a more detailed level. May overlap with other designations. (e.g. energy supply areas)
  • Line designation — indicates transport connections and technical infrastructure networks. Dashed indicative line designations indicate connections/networks whose location or implementation is uncertain. (e.g. power transmission lines)
  • Attribute designation — indicates sub-areas with special characteristics of at least regional significance to be safeguarded or whose constraints must be considered. (e.g. valuable geological formations)
  • Development principle designation — indicates areas and connections significant for the region's desired development that cannot be shown precisely as area reservations or line designations, at a principled level. (e.g. green corridor needs)

Certain designations are subject to an absolute development restriction pursuant to Section 33 of the Land Use Act (AKL), stated in the associated provision. For protected areas, a conditional development restriction applies pursuant to Section 33 of the Land Use Act and Section 49 of the Building Act (in force 1 January 2025).

Energy

DesignationPlanning provision
Power transmission lineA line designation indicates existing power transmission lines of at least 110 kV, as well as connections to be developed within an existing transmission corridor. When planning land use, regulations concerning safety distances for power transmission lines must be taken into account. Subject to a development restriction pursuant to Section 33 of the Land Use Act (AKL).
Indicative alignment of a power transmission lineA dashed line designation indicates indicative alignments of power transmission lines of at least 110 kV planned in a new transmission corridor. In more detailed planning, the feasibility of implementing the alignment must be secured. The location and technical realisation will be specified in more detailed planning and in the formal permitting procedures.
Power transmission line connection needA development principle designation indicates the connection or development need for a power transmission line of at least 110 kV. The location and technical realisation will be specified in more detailed planning.
Energy supply area EN/hA site designation indicates the need and indicative location for a new 400 kV electricity substation essential for the security of electricity transmission supply. When planning land use in the area, the implementation conditions and expansion possibilities of the substation must be safeguarded. The precise location and extent of the area reservation shall be determined in more detailed planning.
Energy production area EN/tA site designation indicates an area for energy production. In planning and implementation, significant environmental disturbances must be prevented through technical solutions and adequate buffer zones. Reserved for facilities, buildings or structures serving energy production and related research and development. A nuclear power plant and interim storage facilities for nuclear waste may be located in the area; supporting functions (temporary accommodation, water-treatment facilities, port operations) may also be located there. In planning, the Radiation and Nuclear Safety Authority (STUK) must be afforded the opportunity to issue an opinion. Precise location and extent shall be determined in more detailed planning.
Indicative protection zone of a nuclear power plantAn attribute designation indicates the approximate location of the protection zone of a nuclear power plant. Exact location and extent shall be determined in further project planning and permitting per STUK's current regulation and case-specific decision. Within the zone, no new dense settlement, hospitals, facilities with significant concentrations of people, or significant production activities affected by a nuclear accident may be planned. Placement of holiday housing/leisure activities must not compromise appropriate rescue operations. STUK and the rescue authority must be afforded the opportunity to issue an opinion.
Indicative alignment of a hydrogen transmission pipelineA dashed line designation indicates the indicative location of a hydrogen transmission pipeline forming part of the national hydrogen infrastructure. Location and technical realisation shall be specified in more detailed planning and permitting. Land reservations must be coordinated with other significant-impact land uses, ensuring a technically and economically feasible alignment remains available. Existing infrastructure corridors shall primarily be utilised to minimise environmental impacts and land-use disadvantages.
Hydrogen transmission pipeline connection needA development principle designation indicates the connection need for a hydrogen transmission pipeline forming part of the national hydrogen infrastructure. Location and technical realisation shall be specified in more detailed planning.
Main gas transmission pipelineA line designation indicates existing high-pressure gas transmission pipelines, intended for natural gas, biogas, synthetic methane or hydrogen and their mixtures. When planning land use, regulations on the safety and safety distances of gas pipelines must be taken into account. Subject to a development restriction pursuant to Section 33 of the Land Use Act (AKL).

Industrial production, circular economy and aggregates supply

DesignationPlanning provision
Concentration of industrial production, logistics, aggregates supply and/or circular economyA development principle designation indicates extensive, regionally significant concentrations of industrial production, logistics, aggregates supply and/or circular economy activities located outside zones for the development of urban functions. Areas are designated for activities that may cause significant environmental impacts. In more detailed planning, the following may be designated: green and clean transition projects; industrial production facilities; facilities handling hazardous chemicals; logistics centres; logistics-intensive industry and supporting functions; aggregates-supply activities; circular economy and waste management; final disposal areas for clean surplus soil; hydrogen production/utilisation projects; carbon dioxide capture projects; and other suitable supporting activities. Location and extent shall be determined so as to form a functional part of a sustainable spatial and settlement structure, taking required infrastructure networks into account. Significant environmental disturbances shall be examined and mitigated through technical solutions and/or adequate buffer zones; environmental risks from storage/manufacture of fuels or hazardous substances shall be considered. No new residential or other incompatible land uses shall be designated; sufficient space for heavy traffic must be reserved. New land use must be adapted to significant environmental, nature, cultural-historical and landscape characteristics. Attention shall be paid to stormwater management and preparedness for extreme weather.

Nature and carbon sequestration

DesignationPlanning provision
Protected areaAn area reservation designation indicates areas protected or intended to be protected under the Nature Conservation Act — national parks, strict nature reserves and other nature conservation areas, and other regionally valuable natural areas — as well as conservation-programme areas and Natura 2000 sites whose implementation is decided under the Act. Together with protected-area designations remaining in force from previous plans, it covers sites of at least 5 ha and more than 200 m in width. May also include an area protected by an authority decision (other than under the Nature Conservation Act) for land under its control; precise boundaries and protection regulations are set out in that decision. Subject to a conditional development restriction pursuant to Section 33 of the Land Use Act (AKL) and Section 49 of the Building Act; not subject to protection provisions under Section 30 of the Land Use Act. No measures may be planned that would endanger or impair the nature and environmental values on which the designation is based.
Need for a green corridorA development principle designation indicates connection needs within the regional ecological and recreational network identified as requiring consideration in more detailed land-use planning. It does not indicate the precise location or width on the ground. In more detailed planning, the connection must be secured so as to primarily ensure species movement, maintain landscape and nature values and, where necessary, support recreational use. The quality and undisturbed nature of green and blue infrastructure shall be ensured, or secondarily the management of disturbances. Where land-use changes weaken the connection, measures for mitigation and for establishing/strengthening an equivalent connection elsewhere must be presented. When determining the precise location, it must be demonstrated that the connection can function as part of a wider ecological and, where necessary, recreational network.
Green corridorA development principle designation indicates connections critical to the functionality of the regional ecological network and for which no alternative locations exist. In more detailed planning, the connection must be secured to primarily ensure species movement, maintain landscape and nature values and, where necessary, support recreational use. The quality and undisturbed nature of green and blue infrastructure shall be ensured, or secondarily the management of disturbances. When determining the precise location, it must be demonstrated that the connection can function as part of a wider ecological and, where necessary, recreational network.
Ecological compensation offset areaAn area reservation designation or corresponding site designation indicates offset areas decided under Section 104 of the Nature Conservation Act (site designations for areas under 50 ha). The primary purpose is ecological compensation under the Act, though multiple land-use forms may be present. As a general principle, produced nature values must not be weakened; detailed restrictions are set out in the decision establishing the offset area. It does not affect the application of the Forest Act. Land-use planning must take into account the restrictions specified in the relevant authority decision.
Ecological development zoneA development principle designation or corresponding site designation indicates areas particularly suitable to be designated as ecological compensation offset areas under the Nature Conservation Act or for improving the state of nature (site designations for areas under 50 ha). It indicates potential and imposes no obligation to implement ecological compensation or nature restoration. Multiple land-use forms may be present. It does not restrict agriculture or forestry, does not affect the Forest Act, and does not preclude development of parts of the area for housing or economic activities. In more detailed planning, opportunities for generating nature values shall be examined.
Area of particular importance for biodiversity (luo)An attribute designation indicates internationally significant bird areas (IBA), nationally significant bird areas (FINIBA), and Finland's ecologically significant underwater marine nature areas (EMMA). Multiple land-use forms may be present; the designation does not restrict use for primary production. Land use must be planned and implemented in a manner that promotes conservation of the underlying nature values and improvement of the state of nature.
Recreational areaAn area reservation designation indicates areas larger than 50 ha for public recreation and outdoor activities, generally on land owned or managed by the state, municipalities or the Uusimaa Recreation Areas Association. It includes existing routes and structures and those needed for consolidating the settlement structure. Subject to a development restriction pursuant to Section 33 of the Land Use Act (AKL). Reserved for public recreation and outdoor activities. In more detailed planning, conditions for recreational use, accessibility, adequate service facilities and environmental values must be safeguarded, with particular attention to environmental quality, the area's position within the ecological network and its significance for biodiversity. Buildings and structures serving public recreational use may be constructed. Based on studies, local routes and technical infrastructure necessary for consolidating the settlement structure may be designated, ensuring unobstructed and safe continuity of recreational connections.
Forest-dominated area (extensive, contiguous, significant for the ecological network)An area reservation designation indicates large and contiguous forest areas primarily used for forestry that are significant for the regional ecological network. In addition to their primary use, areas may serve agriculture and dispersed rural-type development. Planning must safeguard the operating and development conditions of forestry and other rural livelihoods and preserve the integrity of the area by avoiding fragmentation through other land uses creating widespread, permanent or long-term movement barriers relative to the area's size. Based on impact assessment, local routes and technical infrastructure necessary for consolidating the settlement structure may be designated; when planning unavoidable routes, unobstructed and safe continuity of ecological connections must be safeguarded to the greatest possible extent.
Area important for the safeguarding of the cultural environment or landscapeAn attribute designation indicates nationally valuable landscape areas and features (Government Decision 2021), nationally significant built cultural environment areas, roads and sites (RKY 2009), regionally significant cultural environments (Missä maat on mainiommat 2022), and nationally designated landscape management areas (Nature Conservation Act, Section 32). In more detailed spatial planning, construction and land use, the values of nationally significant cultural environments and natural heritage must be safeguarded; regionally significant values must be taken into account when developing areas. Land use per the regional plan's purpose must be assessed and reconciled with the area's landscape and cultural environment values.

Logistics and long-distance passenger transport

Nationally significant single-carriageway roadA line designation indicates mainly single-carriageway roads significant for international and inter-regional traffic. Subject to a development restriction pursuant to Section 33 of the Land Use Act (AKL). No measures may be undertaken on the road or in its immediate vicinity that would weaken the level of service of long-distance traffic, public transport or freight transport. When constructing new junctions, traffic flow and safety must not be compromised. Where indicated in studies and plans, sections may also be dual carriageway.
Regionally significant roadA line designation indicates roads and streets connecting regionally significant centres and functions. Subject to a development restriction pursuant to Section 33 (AKL). No measures may weaken the level of service of long-distance, public or freight transport; new junctions must not compromise traffic flow or safety. In assessing traffic flow, the junction's central position within the spatial and settlement structure may be taken into account.
Sub-regional road of significanceA line designation indicates sub-regionally significant roads and streets, e.g. based on importance for freight or public transport. Same detailed-planning considerations as above (recreation, green corridors, nature, cultural heritage, landscape, water protection, species movement; minimise noise/vibration/emissions).
Alternative indicative alignment of the main railway lineA dashed line designation indicates an alternative indicative alignment of a nationally and regionally significant railway line whose precise location is undetermined. Indicative alignment of a connecting railway lineA dashed line designation indicates the indicative alignment of a connecting railway line whose precise location is undetermined. Alternative alignment of a connecting railway lineA line designation indicates an alternative alignment of a connecting railway line. Same detailed-planning considerations as above. The alignment alternative will be resolved at the plan proposal stage and described in the plan report.
Shipping laneA line designation indicates internationally, nationally or regionally significant shipping lanes. Subject to a development restriction pursuant to Section 33 (AKL).
Transport tunnelA dashed line designation indicates a railway located in a tunnel. Subject to a development restriction pursuant to Section 33 (AKL). The connection must be implemented as an underground railway.

Indicative alignment of a transport tunnelA dashed line designation indicates the indicative alignment of a transport tunnel whose precise location is undetermined. Same detailed-planning considerations as above. The indicative transport tunnel must be implemented as an underground railway.
Alternative indicative alignment of a transport tunnelA dashed line designation indicates an alternative indicative alignment of a transport tunnel where both the precise location and the alternative remain unresolved. Transport area, portAn area reservation designation indicates areas for internationally significant ports. Subject to a development restriction pursuant to Section 33 (AKL). Ports shall be planned so that noise and other environmental disturbances from operations are minimised.
Heavy rail depotA site designation indicates areas intended for depot operations serving heavy rail traffic. Location and extent shall be determined in more detailed planning so the area forms a functionally coherent entity sufficient to safeguard the depot's operational and future development needs, with noise and other disturbances minimised. New land use shall be adapted to the surroundings to safeguard environmental and nature values and take account of cultural-historical and landscape characteristics; attention to stormwater management and preparedness for extreme weather.
Multimodal road–rail terminalA development principle designation indicates an internationally significant multimodal terminal combining road and rail freight transport, enabling transfer between the European standard-gauge and Finnish broad-gauge networks and accommodating heavy-rail depot functions. In the draft plan, three alternative options are presented for the terminal and its rail alignment; the alternative will be resolved at the plan proposal stage and described in the plan report. Location and extent shall be determined so the area is functionally coherent and sufficient to safeguard operational and development conditions, with noise and disturbances minimised. New land use shall be adapted to the surroundings to safeguard environmental, nature and ecological-connection values and take account of cultural-historical and landscape characteristics; attention to stormwater management and extreme-weather preparedness.
Park-and-ride areaA site designation indicates regionally significant park-and-ride areas. In more detailed planning, sufficient space must be reserved and short, safe routes provided from stops to the park-and-ride area and between paired stops. A corresponding service function may also be implemented using alternative technical solutions instead of traditional parking facilities.
Distribution and rest area for alternative fuels for heavy trafficA site designation indicates an area suitable for distributing fuels alternative to fossil fuels for heavy traffic and for a rest area serving heavy traffic. The area must have easy access from a main road, and suitable land must be reservable on a long-term basis for heavy-traffic rest. In implementing fuel-distribution infrastructure, TEN-T (Trans-European Transport Network) requirements shall be taken into account on core and comprehensive network corridors.

DesignationPlanning provision
Indicative alignment of municipal technical servicesA dashed line designation indicates indicative alignments of wastewater tunnels and raw-water tunnels of regional importance for water services. Location and technical realisation shall be specified in connection with the formal permitting procedures. In more detailed planning, the feasibility of implementing the alignment must be ensured.
Connection need for municipal technical servicesA development principle designation indicates connection needs for regional municipal technical services for which no indicative or alternative alignment can be shown. Area for municipal technical servicesA site designation indicates areas for facilities serving municipal technical services, particularly water services. Groundwater areaAn attribute designation indicates groundwater areas important for water supply and classified as suitable for water abstraction, as well as groundwater areas whose safeguarding is necessary for surface water and terrestrial ecosystems. The applicable Uusimaa water management plan and groundwater protection plans must be taken into account; risks threatening groundwater quality and yield shall be reduced. In more detailed planning, particular attention shall be paid to soil and groundwater conditions and to the protection zones of water abstraction facilities; up-to-date information must be verified with the environmental authorities.

National defence

DesignationPlanning provision
Defence Forces areaAn area reservation designation indicates areas in the permanent use of the Defence Forces to which public access is restricted. The area or site is reserved for Defence Forces use.
Defence Forces noise zoneAn attribute designation indicates a noise zone resulting from Defence Forces activities, where shooting and blasting noise exceeds 55 dB LRden or shooting-range noise exceeds 65 dB LAImax. It allows current activity and infill development and major refurbishment of existing buildings. No new residential buildings, holiday homes, care or educational institutions, recreational or camping areas, or other noise-sensitive functions may be located in the area. When planning the use of the area, the Defence Forces must be afforded the opportunity to issue an opinion.
Safety zoneAn attribute designation indicates an area whose use must be restricted due to nearby activities causing danger or significant disturbance. It is based on Government Decree 1101/2015 and the military explosives regulation. Within the delineated safety zone, the placement of new buildings is not permitted. Regarding measures affecting land use and changes in the intended use of buildings, an opinion must be requested from the Defence Forces, who may restrict such changes or measures.
Defence Forces shooting range areaA site designation indicates a shooting range in use by the Defence Forces. The area is reserved for Defence Forces shooting range operations. In more detailed planning and use, the noise impacts on surroundings must be taken into account. Significant environmental disturbances must be prevented through technical solutions and/or adequate buffer zones.
